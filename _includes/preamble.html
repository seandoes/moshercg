The Problem with Agribusiness M&A

Here's the cold, hard truth: mergers & acquisitions (M&A) in the food and agribusiness sectors are tough. M&A is viewed as an accelerant to growth — an express lane — but there is a downside. Once you get on the expressway, things move fast, and there are few off ramps. In fact, many M&A deals destroy more value than they create.

For most executives performing M&A, integration is an afterthought. There are so many moving pieces, they don't know where to start:

  • Do we have a credible plan to deliver the value we promised our board and investors?
  • We are burning cash, what can we do to get back on track?
  • What can we do to ensure our key people don't flock to the competition?
  • We made a big bet. Now how do we avoid making stupid decisions?
  • What risks don't we see and how bad could things get?
  • As the deal sponsor, how do I protect my reputation and my job?
  • What is the glide path to meeting deal objectives with the minimal disruption to our business and customers?

A mishandled M&A integration can set your company back years, undermining performance, financial results, and confidence in leadership. Common symptoms of a botched integration include:

  • Erosion of employee and customer trust
  • Damaged leadership credibility
  • Lost ground to traditional and emerging competitors
  • Capital-consuming deals with subpar returns
